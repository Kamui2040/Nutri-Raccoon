# Contributing

Nutri Raccoon welcomes code, documentation, translation, testing, accessibility, and artwork contributions.

## Workflow

1. Open or reference an issue for substantial work.
2. Create a focused branch from the current `main` branch.
3. Keep commits limited to the intended change.
4. Update tests and relevant public documentation.
5. Open a pull request and complete the checklist.
6. Do not merge directly into `main` outside the documented maintainer workflow.

## Contribution licensing

By submitting a contribution, you confirm that you have the right to submit it and agree that it may be distributed under the licence applying to that part of the project:

- Code: GPL-3.0-or-later.
- Original artwork and documentation: CC BY-SA 4.0.
- Third-party material: only when its licence is compatible and clearly documented.

No separate CLA or DCO is currently required.

## Privacy and safety

Do not submit:

- Real nutrition diaries, body data, or personal health information.
- Database exports from actual users.
- Unedited meal photographs or product photographs containing private surroundings.
- Logs or screenshots containing names, email addresses, account details, device identifiers, or private local paths.
- Keystores, signing passwords, API keys, tokens, credentials, or `.env` files.
- Product datasets or artwork without redistribution permission.

Use synthetic test data and sanitised diagnostics.

## Public identity

Git commits and pull requests are public. Contributors should use a public identity and a privacy-preserving email address where desired.
